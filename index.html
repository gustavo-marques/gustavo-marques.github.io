Physical Oceanographer

I am a Project Scientist at the National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR) working in the Oceanographic Section of the Climate & Global Dynamics (CGD) division. I am part of the team that is coupling MOM6, an open-source ocean model maintained by the Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory , to the Community Earth System Model (CESM). Once this is accomplished, I will serve as the community liaison for the Ocean Model Working Group of CESM.

My research is focused on understanding physical processes controlling the ocean circulation around Antarctica. I am particularly interested in the role that these processes play in regulating the melting of ice shelves and the formation and export of bottom water. I approach these problems by relying on idealized and realistic numerical model simulations that are constrained by observations and theory.
