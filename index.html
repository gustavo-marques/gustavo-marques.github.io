About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Princeton University, working with scientists from the Ocean and Ice-Sheet Processes and the Climate Group at the Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory.

Education

* PhD in Meteorology and Physical Oceanography from the Rosenstiel School of Marine and Atmospheric Science, University of Miami

* MS in Marine Science and Technology at University of Massachusetts Dartmouth

* BS in Oceanography from University of São Paulo

Research Interests

I combine numerical models (hydrostatic and non-hydrostatic), observations and analytical theories to study physical processes in the ocean. Particularly, my research is focused on the role of ocean mixing and turbulent exchange processes around Antarctica in regulating bottom water formation and ice-sheet/ocean interactions. I am also interested in general small-scale buoyancy driven flows, coastal transport processes and operational oceanography.
